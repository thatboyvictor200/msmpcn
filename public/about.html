Our Vision at Mary Slessor Memorial Presbyterian Cathedral

At Mary Slessor Memorial Presbyterian Cathedral, our vision is to raise Christ-like leaders who model His teachings. Our church is dedicated to spreading the love and teachings of Christ. We believe in serving the community, fostering a Godly environment for all and bringing people closer to GOD. This mission is centered around encouraging everyone, everywhere to recognize, maximize, and utilize their God-given potentials for the glory of GOD. We commit to a biblically-based church health model which has an intentional emphasis to create the essential elements of a healthy, growing, biblically-based society.

Our History & Legacy

The Presbyterian Church of Nigeria began in 1846 through the efforts of Scottish missionaries in Calabar, led by Rev. Hope Waddell. Mary Slessor, a Scottish missionary who arrived in 1876, became a renowned advocate for children’s rights and community transformation.

In Port Harcourt, the Mary Slessor Memorial Presbyterian Cathedral was established in the early 1970s by the Port Harcourt Presbytery. Though the name had been chosen earlier by the Aro-Ohafia Presbytery, the Nigerian Civil War delayed the congregation’s formation. After the war, worship resumed at 100 Aggrey Road, and in 1972, Rev. Donald Walker Mackay served as minister.

The parish was officially inaugurated on May 27, 1974, and its dedication held in 1978. It remains a beacon of hope and a strong center of Reformed Presbyterian worship in Rivers State.

  • 1972: Worship resumed at 100 Aggrey Road
  • 1974: Parish officially inaugurated
  • 1978: Church building dedicated
